What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device — computer, tablet, or mobile — by a website when you visit it. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, remember your preferences, and provide information to website owners about how visitors use their site.

This Cookies Policy explains what cookies AmpliSkill uses on aquamarine-platypus-c6116e.netlify.app, why we use them, and how you can control them.

02

How We Use Cookies

AmpliSkill uses cookies for the following purposes:

  • To ensure our website functions correctly and securely.
  • To remember your preferences and settings across sessions.
  • To understand how visitors navigate and use our Site so we can improve it.
  • To measure the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns and event communications.
  • To serve relevant content to users who have previously visited our Site.
03

Categories of Cookies We Use

3.1 — Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the operation of our website. They enable core functions such as page navigation, form submissions, and secure access. These cookies cannot be switched off, as the website cannot function properly without them.

3.2 — Analytics Cookies

We use analytics cookies to collect information about how visitors interact with our Site, such as pages visited, time spent, and navigation paths. This data is aggregated and anonymised, and helps us improve our content and user experience. We use Google Analytics for this purpose.

3.3 — Functional Cookies

Functional cookies allow the website to remember choices you make — such as your location, language, or campaign source — to provide a more personalised experience.

3.4 — Marketing & Targeting Cookies

We may use marketing cookies to deliver content and advertisements relevant to your interests on our Site and third-party platforms (such as LinkedIn). These cookies track your browsing activity across websites.

06

Your Cookie Choices

6.1 — Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our Site, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can update your preferences at any time by clicking the cookie settings link in the footer of our Site.

6.2 — Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can set your browser to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or alert you when cookies are being set. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our Site and your experience.

  • Google Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Cookies and website data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions

6.3 — Opt-Out Tools

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on. You can opt out of LinkedIn personalised advertising through LinkedIn's account settings.

07

Do Not Track

Some browsers support a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not wish to be tracked. Currently, there is no standardised industry response to DNT signals, and our Site does not alter its behaviour based on DNT requests. We encourage you to use the cookie controls described in Section 6 to manage your preferences.
