Editorial mission

Rigour you can use.

AJMSI publishes research that practitioners read and practice that researchers cite. We welcome empirical studies, original research, rigorous essays, and practice notes that advance how organizations are led, strategized, and built.

Our review process combines academic peer review with practitioner validation. Accepted papers are published in print and open-access digital formats — and, where relevant, distilled into teaching notes for use in executive education.

How to submit

What we publish.

AJMSI accepts five types of submissions. All are peer-reviewed. All are welcome from both academic and industry-affiliated authors.

Authors should submit a blinded manuscript, a cover letter, and a short author statement. Submissions are via our editorial portal (link on launch).

Queries and pre-submission inquiries are welcome and encouraged.

Pre-submission inquiry →
  • Leading Article
    8,000–10,000 WORDS · PEER REVIEWED
    Substantial, agenda-setting pieces that advance thinking on a major theme in management, strategy, or innovation.
  • Empirical Research
    6,000–10,000 WORDS · PEER REVIEWED
    Quantitative, qualitative, or mixed-method studies with clear research questions, methodology, and findings.
  • Practice Note
    2,500–4,000 WORDS · PEER REVIEWED
    Field observations from practitioners — patterns, anti-patterns, and lessons from real organizational work.
  • Case Study
    4,000–8,000 WORDS · PEER REVIEWED
    Teaching-focused case studies with accompanying teaching note. Suitable for executive education use.
  • Essay & Review
    1,500–3,500 WORDS · EDITORIAL REVIEW
    Thoughtful, well-argued essays on emerging management topics. Book reviews of 1,500 words max.
